Events that Have Shaped Me

Social Activism
Jan 1980 - Present

Since a young age, I have always wanted to fix social inequalities and make the world into a place where old people don't have to suffer. That's kind of hard to do when there are bombs falling on Tehran and killing my friends and family members, but I have always persevered. Throughout my life I have made sure I have kept a positive mindset whenever addressing issues, by attending protests, I was always politically active and knowledge.

Love for Family
Mar 1985 - Present

When I left Iran and relocated to Europe I constantly felt homesick. Eventually I realized I had to go back to Iran. Giving up my freedom was hard, but living with my family was what I needed. My mom and dad treated me as an equal person regardless of gender, and my grandmother gave me the reality check that I needed: I had to always be true to myself in order to be happy.

My Identity
Jun 1975 - Present

When in Iran, I continued doing whatever social activism I could. I also supported my family around, but eventually started to find my inner purpose. All these things were steps to find my identity. I wanted to make sure future generations were all treated equall. After a couple of years of this, I realized that Iran was not for me, and moved back to Europe. I finally realized that freedom had a price when I was held back.
